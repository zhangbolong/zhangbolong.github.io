USS Midway (CV-41)

A revolutionary aircraft carrier of the United States Navy

USS Midway sailing through the Western Pacific in November 1974

Service history
  • Midway was laid down 27 October 1943 by Newport News Shipbuilding Co., Newport News, Virginia
  • Launched 20 March 1945, sponsored by Mrs. Bradford William Ripley, Jr.
  • Commissioned on 10 September 1945 (eight days after the Surrender of Japan) with Captain Joseph F. Bolger in command.
  • From 20 February 1946, it was the flagship for Carrier Division 1.
  • On 29 October 1947, Midway sailed for the first of its annual deployments with the 6th Fleet in the Mediterranean.
  • On June 1951, Midway operated in the Atlantic off the Virginia Capes during carrier suitability tests of the F9F-5 Panther.
  • In 1952, the ship participated in Operation Mainbrace, North Sea maneuvers with NATO forces.
  • Midway cleared Norfolk 27 December 1954 for a world cruise, sailing via the Cape of Good Hope for Taiwan, where it joined the 7th Fleet for operations in the Western Pacific until 28 June 1955.
  • In August 1991, Midway departed Yokosuka and returned to Pearl Harbor. This is her final cruise
